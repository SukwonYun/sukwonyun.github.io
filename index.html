Bio

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Tianlong Chen. I was also a research intern at Regev Lab, Genentech, working with Prof. Aviv Regev and Dr. Russell Littman. Prior to UNC, I obtained an M.S. in ISysE at KAIST, advised by Prof. Chanyoung Park. During Master's, I spent wonderful time in Tokyo as a visiting researcher at Tokyo Tech, working with Prof. Tsuyoshi Murata and, Dr. Xin Liu.

My current research interests lie in AI4Science and Multi-Agent LLMs. Specifically, I am interested in (A) Multimodal learning through the lens of MoE and GNNs, with applications in Computational Biology. Additionally, I have recently been focusing on (B) Multi-Agent LLMs, exploring communication strategies to achieve collaborative synergy. Throughout my research journey, I have received 3 Best Paper Awards (AAAI 2024 GenAI4Health (MoE+AD), KDD 2024 FedKDD (Graph), ICML 2023 WCB (Graph+Single-cell)), as well as 1 Spotlight Paper (NeurIPS 2024 (MoE)) and 1 Oral Paper (ICLR 2025 (Graph)).
